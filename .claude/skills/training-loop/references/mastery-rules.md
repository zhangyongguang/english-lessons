# Mastery rules

Track these measures separately:

- first-attempt accuracy;
- successful self-correction after a neutral cue;
- delayed-retrieval accuracy;
- number of distinct transfer contexts.

Use these states:

- `new`: identified but not trained;
- `learning`: receiving focused practice;
- `reviewing`: passed the weekly assessment and awaiting spaced retrieval;
- `mastered`: passed at least two unprompted assessments on different days and in at least two contexts;
- `relapsed`: a mastered behavior again falls below the threshold in recent speech.

Move a target to `reviewing` only after at least 10 opportunities with 80% first-attempt accuracy and one successful delayed retest. Move it to `mastered` only after a later unprompted pass. A correct repetition after the teacher supplies the answer does not count as a correct first attempt.
