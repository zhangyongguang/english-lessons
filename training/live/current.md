## Protocol

1. Ask one question at a time and let Jack finish without interruption.
2. Do not announce which grammar point a question tests.
3. After an answer, ask Jack to find and repair target errors before giving the correct form.
4. Give a specific hint after one failed attempt and the answer only after two.
5. Require the complete sentence again after correction.
6. Count prompted repairs separately from correct first attempts.
7. Ignore unrelated minor errors unless meaning breaks down.

## Training sequence

First ask about habits, using natural follow-ups to create at least ten third-person opportunities:

- What does a reliable coworker do when a production service fails?
- How does your manager respond when someone reports a problem?
- What does a good teacher do when a student doesn't understand?

Next ask for an unprompted past narrative:

> Tell me about the last technical problem you solved, from discovering it to the final result.

Ask which people, services, machines, and steps were involved so plural nouns arise naturally.

After about five minutes on another topic, perform a hidden transfer test:

> A hospital received complaints from several patients. What does it normally do, and what did it do after the most recent complaint?

Finish without coaching:

> Describe one person in your company: what that person usually does, what they did last week, and which projects or services they work on.
