# Weekly Speaking Training · 2026-W28

## Focus

This week trains only three foundational forms that repeatedly break down during real-time speech. The evidence window is 2026-W26 through 2026-W28; W28 currently contains lessons from July 6, July 9, July 10, and July 11.

## 1. Past tense in completed-event narratives

**Trigger:** talking about yesterday, a previous trip, or another completed event.

**Evidence:** 12 `tense` errors across all four W28 lesson days; 41 errors across 15 lesson days in the three-week window.

**Real patterns:**

- “I eat some food.” → “I ate some food.”
- “I forgot what I eat.” → “I forgot what I ate.”
- “I ride a taxi from the airport.” → “I rode a taxi from the airport.”
- “Maybe they met this question before.” → “Maybe they have encountered this problem before.”

**Training cue:** establish the time first, then keep completed actions in the past.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, followed by one correct delayed retest in a different context.

## 2. Plural marking on countable nouns

**Trigger:** referring to two or more countable people, objects, places, services, or responsibilities.

**Evidence:** 10 `plural` errors across three W28 lesson days; 33 errors across 13 lesson days in the three-week window.

**Real patterns:**

- “some pastry” → “some pastries”
- “you and your neighbor” → “you and your neighbors”
- “skills about website” → “skills about websites”
- “some breads” → “some bread”
- “many useful information” → “some useful information”
- “some video” → “some videos”

**Training cue:** when a quantity or general group is mentioned, check whether the noun needs plural marking.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, including regular plurals and one irregular or zero-ending case such as `people`.

## 3. Third-person subject–verb agreement

**Trigger:** using the present tense with `he`, `she`, `it`, one person, one organization, or `someone`.

**Evidence:** 9 `agreement` errors across all four W28 lesson days; 28 errors across 13 lesson days in the three-week window.

**Real patterns:**

- “someone who drink a lot” → “someone who drinks a lot”
- “KFC provide local food” → “KFC provides local food”
- “someone who ... don't trouble me” → “someone who ... doesn't trouble me”
- “my country import oil” → “my country imports oil”
- “if a person build connections” → “if a person builds connections”
- “unless my daughter want to get one” → “unless my daughter wants to get one”
- “it just waste my time” → “it just wastes my time”

**Training cue:** identify the subject before selecting `do/does` or adding third-person singular `-s`.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, including affirmative verbs and `doesn't`.

## Five-session progression

| Session | Main purpose | Prompt support |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Free production, notice, self-correction, complete restatement | Moderate |
| Day 2 | Retrieve the same forms in new questions | Minimal |
| Day 3 | Transfer to work, healthcare, and community topics | Minimal |
| Day 4 | Mix all three targets under follow-up pressure | None unless communication fails |
| Day 5 | Unprompted assessment with unseen questions | None |

## Weekly decision rule

A target may enter spaced review only after at least 80% first-attempt accuracy, one delayed retest, and use in two contexts. Immediate repetition after hearing the answer does not count as mastery.
