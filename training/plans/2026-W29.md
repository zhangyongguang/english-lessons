# Weekly Speaking Training · 2026-W29

## Focus

This week keeps the same three foundational targets because they have not become automatic in free speech. The evidence window is 2026-W27 through 2026-W29; W29 contains the July 14, July 15, and July 16 lessons.

## 1. Third-person subject–verb agreement

**Trigger:** using the simple present with `he`, `she`, `it`, one person, one company, or one organization.

**Why now:** this target remains in learning after 40% first-attempt accuracy in the first live session. W29 added 10 errors across two lesson days; the three-week window contains 31 errors across 11 days.

**Real patterns:**

- “One of my coworkers need to go to different places.” → “One of my coworkers needs to go to different places.”
- “Her mom sent her to school most times.” → “Her mom sends her to school most of the time.”
- “The manager need to check their tasks.” → “The manager needs to check their tasks.”

**Training cue:** identify the singular subject before choosing `do/does` or adding third-person `-s`.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, one correct delayed retest, and correct use in two transfer contexts.

## 2. Past tense in completed-event narratives

**Trigger:** talking about yesterday, an earlier stage of life, a completed activity, or completed research.

**Why now:** this target remains in learning after 75% first-attempt accuracy. W29 added 8 tense errors across all three lesson days; the three-week window contains 33 errors across 12 days.

**Real patterns:**

- “I had a good day today, stay at home, so I have enough time.” → “I had a good day today. I stayed at home, so I had enough time.”
- “During that time my salary is not high. I only have 100,000 yuan.” → “At that time, my salary was not high. I only had 100,000 yuan.”
- “I searched many places. I find Japan and the UK...” → “I searched many places. I found that Japan and the UK...”

**Training cue:** establish the past time first, then keep each completed action or state in the past.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, one correct delayed retest, and correct use in two transfer contexts.

## 3. Plural marking on countable nouns

**Trigger:** referring to two or more countable people, objects, places, events, services, or responsibilities.

**Why now:** although this target had reached reviewing, W29 added 20 plural errors across advertising, work, technology, travel, and family topics. The three-week window contains 42 errors across 11 days.

**Real patterns:**

- “I can focus on their product and achievement.” → “I can focus on their products and achievements.”
- “One of my coworker needs to go to different places.” → “One of my coworkers needs to go to different places.”
- “If you have too much business trip...” → “If you have too many business trips...”

**Training cue:** after a number, `many`, `some`, or `one of`, check whether the countable noun needs a plural ending.

**Pass condition:** at least 8 correct first attempts out of 10 opportunities, one correct delayed retest, and correct use in two transfer contexts.

Use `training/live/current.md` as the complete 15–20 minute lesson prompt. Track only these three targets, count first attempts separately from prompted repairs, include delayed retrieval, and finish with the required Training report.

## Weekly decision rule

A target may enter spaced review only after at least 80% first-attempt accuracy, one delayed retest, and use in two contexts. Immediate repetition after hearing the answer does not count as mastery.
